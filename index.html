Projects

Aerodynamics of Tandem Flapping Wings

BIAHL, IIT Kharagpur, West Bengal, India

Dragonflies take one of the most impressive flights when it comes to insects. The expertise in high maneuverability along with optimal lift-to-thrust efficiency makes the wings of a dragonfly an inspiring concept for an aerodynamicist. This project is focussed on the numerical and experimental analysis of a flapping wing.

Spray Analysis and Turbulence Characterization in a Two-fluid Atomizer

LEGI, Université Grenoble Alpes, Grenoble, France

Atomizers play a cardinal role in innumerable research & industrial applications. The spray physics and two-fluid flow physics associated with atomizers is a deciding facet in places like jet engines, boilers, etc. This project is based on spray parametrisation & turbulence characterisation.

Aerodynamic Design of an Expendable UAV

Aeronautical Development Establishment, DRDO, Bangalore, India

Aircraft Design is the process used by aerospace engineers to design an aerial vehicle with established design specifications. The entire process can even take as long as 10 years! This is because the process is highly iterative and involves the teamwork of all spheres of aerospace. This project was done at the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE), DRDO. I worked on the Aerodynamic Design of an Expendable UAV on MATLAB and OpenVSP.

Formula Student Combustion Vehicle

TeamKART, IIT Kharagpur, West Bengal, India

TeamKART is the official formula student team of IIT Kharagpur. We are a group of students who love to get our hands dirty, and design, manufacture and test formula-student style prototype race cars. I am the head of Chassis Subsystem of the team and I lead a group of 8 junior undergraduate students.

Boeing India Mentorship Program

Boeing & IIT Kharagpur (Joint Program), India

I was selected for a joint program by Boeing and IIT Kharagpur in August 2021. The aim of the project is to help the selected students develop understanding of various technological advances in today's industry by assigning them a mentor from Boeing. The mentor can provide exposure to the student through various tasks, presentations, and discussions. The duration of the project is nine months.

Design of a Small Scale Shocktube

Aerodynamics Laboratory, IIT Kharagpur, India

A shocktube is a tube in which experimental shock waves are produced as a result of the rupturing of a diaphragm separating two chambers containing a gas or gases at differential pressure. This project focusses on building a real scaled down shocktube with future targets of studying shock interaction with water lamina flowing down a ramp.

Study of Fish-Inspired Propulsion

Propulsion Laboratory, IIT Kharagpur, India

Fishes can produce thrust forces by virtue of their oscillatory and undulating body-tail motion without the need of a dedicated powerplant. They can achieve high maneuverability at the expense of less energy. This project is done as a semester term project and revolves around the numerical and experimental study of fish tail propulsion.

About me

Undergraduate Student • Aerospace Enthusiast • Traveller • Badminton Player

Hi! My name is Akashdeep Singh. I am currently doing my bachelors in Aerospace Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, India. Although I am always curious to learn anything that life has to offer, my specific interests in Engineering lie in Experimental Fluid Dynamics, Turbulence, and Bio-Inspired Aerodynamics. Through TeamKART - the Official Formula Student Team of IIT Kharagpur, I was introduced to Formula Racing Engineering. I cultivated interest and got hands-on experience in manufacturing processes. Towards the start of my third-year, through my project on tandem flapping wing aerodynamics, I delved deeper into complex fluid interactions, turbulence, and CFD. To get more headway into turbulence and experimental fluid dynamics, I applied for a scholarship for a research internship and I was awarded the same. My internship was chaperoned by an additional touch of Multiphase Fluid Dynamics and Hot-Wire Experiments. Before I dived into fluid dynamics, I got my first industry experience through my internship at ADE, DRDO, Bangalore in Aircraft Design. Through my other projects towards the middle and end of my third year, I was introduced to supersonic fluid dynamics and also fish propulsion. The projects accorded me with more valuable knowledge as well as team skills. My list of experiences also include a direct interaction programme with a Boeing mentor on various industry techniques and exposure through projects and tasks.

Besides Aerospace, I have a zeal towards travelling, exploring different cultures, trying out new cuisines, and connecting with new people. The thought of scouting around and discovering life through a new lens has always been invigorating to me. Things that I do when I am not travelling or inside my 'Aerospace Kingdom' include playing different sports, listening to music, chatting with friends, or just laughing and living my best life. At the end of the day, I would like to gain a polished scientific lens with an artistic prowess.
